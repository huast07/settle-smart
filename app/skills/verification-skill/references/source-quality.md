# Source Quality Rules

Use this reference when deciding whether a discovered program is reliable enough to recommend.

## Strong Evidence

- Official provider signup page.
- Official app store or provider help page explaining enrollment.
- Government, transit agency, utility, or public program page with current eligibility.
- Government health agency or benefits portal page with current healthcare or prescription benefit eligibility.
- Bank, issuer, retailer, pharmacy, grocery chain, telecom carrier, or internet provider page naming the offer.

## Medium Evidence

- Official press release without detailed signup instructions.
- Reputable local news article that points to an official program.
- Aggregator page that links to an official signup or program page.

## Weak Evidence

- Blog posts without dates.
- Forum/social posts.
- Coupon pages that do not link to the provider.
- Pages about a different city, province/state, or country.

## Confidence Labels

- `high`: official source confirms program and signup path.
- `medium`: official source confirms program, but eligibility or signup steps need verification.
- `low`: evidence suggests the program exists, but source, location, or current availability is uncertain.
