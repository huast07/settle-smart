# Program Ranking Rubric

Score candidate programs qualitatively using these factors.

## Fit

- Matches the user's actual destination and service area.
- Covers a high-priority essential category.
- Fits age and household composition.
- Does not require assumptions about immigration status, income, or credit unless the source says so.

## Value

- Reduces recurring costs or unlocks essential access.
- Has low or no enrollment cost.
- Has practical rewards, discounts, or fee waivers.

## Friction

- Clear signup path.
- Online or easy in-person enrollment.
- Low documentation burden.
- No hard credit check unless clearly worth flagging.

## Evidence

- High confidence official source outranks weak third-party evidence.
- Recent/current pages outrank undated pages.
- Exact local page outranks national page when benefits differ by region.

## Recommendation Balance

Rank programs inside each essential goods or service category. Recommend the top three credible programs per category when evidence supports three choices. If fewer than three credible programs exist, return fewer and explain uncertainty through confidence and caveats. Avoid filling a category with weak or invented programs just to reach three.
